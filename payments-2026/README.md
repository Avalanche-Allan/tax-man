## How to pay (IRS Direct Pay — free, no account needed)

Make **two separate payments** of $3,444.53 each (one covers Q1, one
covers Q2) so you get one receipt per quarter.

1. Go to <https://www.irs.gov/payments/direct-pay> → "Make a payment"
2. **Reason for Payment:** `Estimated Tax`
3. **Apply Payment To:** `1040-ES` (it auto-selects)
4. **Tax Period for Payment:** `2026`  ← the most common mistake is
   picking the wrong year. It must say 2026.
5. Identity verification: choose a tax year you've already FILED
   (use **2024** — 2025 may not be processed yet). Enter your name,
   SSN, DOB, filing status, and address **exactly as they appeared on
   that year's return** — for a foreign address, character-for-
   character as filed, or verification fails.
6. Bank info: US checking account routing + account number
   (foreign banks don't work with Direct Pay).
7. Amount: `3444.53`. Payment date: today.
8. On the confirmation page, note the confirmation number and
   **save the page as PDF into this folder**.
9. Repeat steps 1-8 for the second payment.

Then tell Claude the dates/amounts so they get recorded in the 2026
session (`a09cd89bb104`) — they go on Form 1040 line 26 next spring.

## About the missed Q1

Nothing extra to do now — just pay it. The late-payment cost is an
underpayment penalty computed on Form 2210 with the 2026 return
(roughly $35–45 for ~2 months late at the current ~7% annual rate).
Paying today stops it from growing. Do NOT add the penalty to the
payment; the IRS calculates it at filing time.
